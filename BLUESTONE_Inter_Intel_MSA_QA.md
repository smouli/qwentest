A1: No, Vendor shall not sub-contract its Processing obligations performed on behalf of the Client under the Agreement to a sub-processor without the prior written consent of the Client.

Q2: What terms must be included in sub-contracting agreements?
A2: Such sub-contracting shall only be permitted by way of a written agreement which includes terms which are the same or equivalent to those set out in this Exhibit.

Q3: Is Client consent required for subcontracting?
A3: Yes, Vendor must obtain the Client's prior written consent before subcontracting Processing obligations.

## SECTION 7.2 — DEEMED AUTHORIZATION FOR SUB-PROCESSORS

Q1: When is Vendor deemed authorized to engage a sub-processor?
A1: Vendor shall be deemed authorized by the Client to engage a subprocessor (and this Exhibit shall be deemed to be a written authorization) where: (a) Vendor makes a written notification to the Client prior to engaging the sub-processor which contains the name and address of the sub-processor, the role such sub-processor will have in Processing the Client Data, the location from which the sub-processor will provide its services, the nature of the Client Data that will be processed by the sub-processor and in relation to which Data Subjects; and (b) the Client does not object to Vendor engaging such sub-processor within 10 days of receiving such notification.

Q2: What information must Vendor provide in the notification?
A2: Vendor must provide: the name and address of the sub-processor, the role such sub-processor will have in Processing the Client Data, the location from which the sub-processor will provide its services, the nature of the Client Data that will be processed by the sub-processor and in relation to which Data Subjects.

Q3: How long does the Client have to object?
A3: The Client has 10 days from receiving the notification to object to Vendor engaging such sub-processor.

Q4: What happens if the Client does not object within 10 days?
A4: If the Client does not object within 10 days, Vendor is deemed authorized to engage the sub-processor.

## SECTION 8.1 — PRIVACY SHIELD

Q1: What privacy protection standards must Vendor meet?
A1: To the extent not provided for above, Vendor will provide at least the same levels of privacy protection as are mandated and required under the EU-US and Swiss-US Privacy Shield Framework Agreements maintained by the Department of Commerce ("Privacy Shield").

Q2: How must Vendor Process Client Data?
A2: Vendor will Process any Client Data in a manner consistent with the Client's obligations under Privacy Shield including, without limitation, its obligations with respect to onward transfer.

Q3: What Privacy Shield frameworks are referenced?
A3: The EU-US and Swiss-US Privacy Shield Framework Agreements maintained by the Department of Commerce are referenced.
